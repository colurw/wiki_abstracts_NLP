BOYLE LECTURES
 The Boyle Lectures are named after Robert Boyle, a prominent natural philosopher of the 17th
century and son of Richard Boyle, 1st Earl of Cork.  Under the terms of his Will, Robert Boyle
endowed a series of lectures or sermons (originally eight each year) which were to consider the
relationship between Christianity and the new natural philosophy (today's 'science') then emerging
in European society.
